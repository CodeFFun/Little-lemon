NRCS contributed to ending water woes

Queuing with empty water containers, arguments to fill water, and inadequate and unhygienic water supply used to be a common sight at Ward-8 of Rhising Rural Municipality, Tanahu three months ago. People had to queue for hours or fetch water in containers from other water sources as the community tap provided inadequate water supply to its households in the area. Water scarcity has limited access to safe water for drinking, practicing health hygiene, and irrigation in the community. Because of unsafe water, has tempted water-borne diseases like typhoid, diarrhea, and cholera in the community, especially during the onset of monsoon and summer.

However, the situation has changed at present. Inhabitants of Ward-8 of Rishing RM have never imagined having a tap in their houses. The installation of individual taps in households has changed the destiny of the village. A regular supply of water has not only distributed safer drinking water but at the same time has helped to improve health, education, and economic, and social condition in the community.

In thirsty Rhising RM, villagers have to race against each other to get enough water. While most people are sleeping, women in Rhising's Ward-8 village get up in the middle of the night to fetch water from a neighboring community tap.

Before the installation of the tap, I used to wake up around 4 am to collect water every day. During the dry season, it was even harder for us as we have to wake up at midnight to fetch water for household chores, said Ms. Dil Maya Thapa, a local of Ward-8. In another word, collecting enough water for my family was the only priority in my life. Women require more water than men, and there would be situations where I didnt have any water to flush even in the toilet. Because of this many people suffered from waterborne diseases such as diarrhea and cholera as well. Women from our community used to gather and go together to collect water at midnight because we are scared to go alone. Even we were scared we dont have any option not to go, added Dil Maya who has also been working as a treasure of the Consumer Water Supply Committee at Ward-8.

We will deploy the guard when every tap gets the water meters fitted.

She said that she has no more helping hands in her house as her husband is a migrant worker in Qatar and she was forced to collect water by herself. But the situation changed after the Nepal Red Cross Society (NRCS) installed the tap and supplied the drinking water. Now our daily routine has changed. Now, I dont have to be the early bird. I am happy that I am getting enough sleep now which I felt that has increased my productivity. Even I am devoting my time to gardening some vegetables in the backyard of my house and taking care of my son. We are able to drink clean water, eat and sleep on time, clean our houses, use the water for farming, etc.

The water supply has been initiated three months ago although; it was constructed by the Nepal Red Cross Society much earlier; it was destroyed by the flood. Houses in our area have collected money and saved it in the fund to pay the guard who operates the water tank. As we do not have a guard now, we have put the money in the bank and will use it if we have to repair any taps. We will deploy the guard when every tap gets the water meters fitted.

Championing for Change: A Father's fight for the Dignity of His Daughter

I named my daughter 'Manisha,' meaning 'goddess of eternity,' symbolizing intelligence and desire. However, because of her physical condition, she has been subjected to hurtful and negative stereotypes her entire life, causing immense pain to me as a parent. She cant speak properly, and some people even mocked her., shared Dhurba Pariyar, Manishas father and Chairperson of Bansara Self-help group, Dordi Rural Municipality - 8, Lamjung.

Manisha holds a red card indicating complete disability which was provided to her according to Nepal's Act on the Rights of Persons with Disability 2074 for identification indicating different levels of disabilities. He added, Before I used to passively tolerate this, and I did not realize how much this was hurting my daughters self-esteem and dignity. But after participating in training sessions conducted by the Bansara Self-Help Group initiated by the Nepal Red Cross Society (NRCS), I have learned about the rights of people with disabilities and the impact of language on their self-esteem.

Bansara Self-Help Group became involved in the Empowered Persons with disabilities in an Inclusive and Resilient Community (EPIC) project which is being implemented by the Nepal Red Cross Society (NRCS) with the goal to enhance the empowerment of Persons with Disabilities by increasing their knowledge and awareness, ensuring their access to services and information, and strengthening their disaster resilience.

He proudly added, Through community programs and meetings, I have successfully raised awareness and changed the negative perception of people with disabilities in my community. The people of the community became aware of their rights and dignity.

Today, my daughter is also aware of her rights. She is also being called by her name. I realized that this small change has made her much happier and more confident.

Changing societal attitudes and behaviors is difficult, but the training and orientations have yielded positive results in my community. While we occasionally still encounter derogatory language from older generations, overall, there has been a significant shift in how people with disabilities are perceived and treated.

Balancing my professional life with my passion for social work, I am committed to improving the lives of individuals living with disabilities. As a parent of a child with disabilities, I understand the daily struggles they face and feel a strong sense of urgency to help. Through my work with the Bansara self-help group, I am advocating for increased support and resources for the vulnerable and marginalized people in my community. I am also collaborating with fellow members to raise awareness among political leaders and encourage the development of policies aimed at promoting the socio-economic growth of people with disabilities.
